About

Growing up as I did in an artistic family, I absorbed many years of design thinking. In the beginning, I cared most about aesthetics. Is it beautiful? Is it visually appealing? Over time, I developed a more sophisticated and holistic way of interpreting and evaluating the design of things. I like to take an object and mull over it. How might I change the design of things to make them more user-friendly, efficient, graceful? I like to take complex things and break them down into their component parts and processes.

As a designer, I am not just concerned with the finished product; some of the most interesting design decisions concern intermediate processes that the end user never sees. Given my lifelong inclination towards design, it is no surprise that for my undergraduate major I chose industrial design.
